# solar-smart-bus-stop
Solar-powered smart bus stop integrating Wi-Fi, safety features, and sustainable energy solutions for urban infrastructure

**Overview**
This project presents a sustainable and intelligent public transport infrastructure solution designed to improve safety, accessibility, and digital connectivity at urban bus stops. It integrates solar energy with smart features to create a self-sufficient and user-friendly system.

**Problem Statement**
Conventional bus stops in India lack essential facilities such as lighting, safety systems, real-time information, and shelter. This makes them unsafe and inconvenient, especially for women, children, and the elderly. Additionally, reliance on grid electricity increases operational costs and carbon footprint.

**Solution**
The proposed system is a solar-powered smart bus stop that operates independently of the grid while integrating multiple smart features like Wi-Fi, CCTV, real-time display, and emergency support systems.

**Key Features**
Solar-powered energy system (off-grid)
Public Wi-Fi connectivity
CCTV surveillance for safety
Panic button for emergency situations
LED lighting with automation
USB charging ports
Real-time digital display for bus schedules
EV charging capability
Seating capacity for 10 people with covered shelter
Accessibility features (ramps, inclusive design)

**Technical Details**
Daily energy requirement: 21.3 kWh/day
Solar panel capacity: ~5 kW installed
Battery storage: ~42.6 kWh (2-day backup)
System uses MPPT controller for efficient energy management
Fully functional during both day (solar-powered) and night (battery-powered)

**Cost & Payback**
Estimated cost: ₹1.87 lakh
With subsidy: ₹1.1 lakh
Monthly savings + revenue: ₹2000
Payback period: ~4.6 years (with subsidy)

**Impact**
Reduces carbon emissions (~700 kg CO₂/year)
Improves public safety and accessibility
Promotes digital inclusion
Supports:
SDG 7 (Clean Energy)
SDG 9 (Infrastructure)
SDG 11 (Sustainable Cities)

**Implementation Scope**
Suitable for major Indian cities like Bengaluru, Delhi, Mumbai, Pune, Hyderabad, and Chennai where public transport demand and smart city initiatives are growing.
